About Me:
I currently reside in the Central Valley, which is a perfect match for my main
hobbies of hiking and fishing. As the name implies, I am central most to activities
and places in California. There are beautiful hiking and fishing spots all within
2-3 hours of where I live. If I want to travel in-state, Los Angeles is only 3-4 hours
away. San Francisco, 1-2 hours. Yosemite, 2-3 hours. These are things residents of Central
Valley tell themselves to justify their existence and when they want to sell their house
to the next victim. My only future goal is to basically travel the world. It might seem a bit
dramatic but every step and breath I take, must get me closer to realizing that dream.
Why? So, about a month before the pandemic, I got the opportunity to travel around Europe for
about 3 weeks. I basically picked a spot on the map and headed off. My single rule was to only
plan one new destination at a time. I started off in Lisbon, Portugal. After a couple days,
I headed off to Valencia, Spain. I then hopped on a train to Barcelona, Spain. All together,
I also visited Maranello (Forza Scuderia Ferrari!) and Rome, Italy; Paris, France; London, UK.
I could write a whole book about my experience backpacking in Europe, but better writers have already
done that. The main takeaway for me was, even though all those places had their unique traits, customs,
culture, etc.. I still felt a certain level of similarity, familiarity, and wholenessirity (not a word yet)
of the human experience. I fell in love with that experience and now my life mission is to relive those
moments everyday for the rest of my life.
